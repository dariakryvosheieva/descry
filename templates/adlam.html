About the Script

Adlam is an alphabet used to write the Fulani language spoken in West Africa. It was invented in 1989 by two Guinean brothers, Abdoulaye and Ibrahima Barry, when they were children[1]. It also stands for the first four letters of the alphabet: A, D, L, and M[2].

Adlam is written right to left and includes 28 common letters + 6 supplemental letters used only in loanwords. Uppercase and lowercase letters are distinguished. Additionally, Adlam has its own diacritics, digits, and punctuation marks.

The script has been supported by Unicode since 2016[3]. In 2019, the shapes of the letters were revised for purposes of convenience and legibility[4]. Currently there are three font families for the script: Google's Noto Sans Adlam[5], Adobe's Kigelia Adlam[6], and Microsoft's ADLaM Display[3].

In 2022, Microsoft issued a series of typographic artworks in celebration of the creation of ADLaM Display[3]

This project focuses on letters in their post-2019 shapes. Since uppercase and lowercase letters are often distinguished only by size, they are classified into the same output class.
