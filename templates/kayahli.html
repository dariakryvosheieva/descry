1. Introduction

Kayah Li is an alphabet used to write dialects of the Kayah continuum (also known as Karenni or Red Karen), belonging to the Karenic branch of the Sino-Tibetan language family and spoken in Myanmar. It was invented in 1962 by Htae Bu Phae, a teacher from southeastern Myanmar, to underscore Karen people's specificity against Burmese people[1].

Kayah Li is written left to right and includes 28 base letters, 5 common vowels represented as ꤢ with diacritics, and 5 diphthongs occuring only in loanwords, represented as ꤣ with diacritics. Additionally, there are tone markers and digits.
